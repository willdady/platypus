# Platypus

Platypus is a full-stack platform for building and managing AI agents — agents
that reason, use tools, and connect to your data through the Model Context
Protocol. It's multi-tenant, multi-provider, and self-hosted: you bring the
models, Platypus gives you everything around them.

These docs are the source of truth for narrative content — setup, the domain
model, build guides, and extension points. Pick a starting point below, or use
the navigation to find your way around.

## Find your path

The docs are organised around what you're trying to do:

- **Just want to see it run?** [Getting Started](/getting-started) takes you from
  nothing to your first streamed reply with Docker Compose and a five-step loop.
- **Want to understand the model?** [Concepts](/concepts) explains the domain in
  plain language — Organizations, Workspaces, Chats, Agents, Skills, MCP,
  Sandboxes, and Memory — and why things are arranged the way they are.
- **Building agents?** [Building with Platypus](/building-with-platypus) is the
  task-oriented guide for Workspace Owners: configure an Agent, give it Skills
  and MCP tools, and put it to work with Triggers, Boards, and Dashboards.
- **Running the platform?** [Self-Hosting](/self-hosting) is for Operators —
  deployment, configuration, providers and authentication, and the sandbox
  infrastructure.
- **Working in the codebase?** [Extending](/extending) maps the in-tree
  extension points (sandbox backends and tool sets) and the decisions behind
  them.
- **Need an exact value?** [Reference](/reference) enumerates every
  configuration option as flat, lookup-oriented tables.
